AI Basics, the cheat sheet

The four-part prompt

  1. Role: who the AI should be. "You are an experienced internal-communications writer." (Dr. Jules White's Persona Pattern)
  2. Context: what it can't guess. Who this is for, what they know, what's at stake.
  3. Task: the one deliverable, plus its job. "Draft an email that explains the timeline and reassures the team."
  4. Format: shape, tone, length. "Three short paragraphs, warm and direct, under 200 words."

Then iterate: correct it like a new hire. "Shorter." "Warmer." "As a table."

What can you paste? 🟢🟡🔴

🟢 Green, any approved tool: public info, generic questions, drafts with no names or internal details.
🟡 Yellow, Amplify or VU-signed Copilot only: internal plans, processes, work drafts with VU context.
🔴 Red, never in any AI tool: FERPA, HIPAA, salaries and personnel, donor data, credentials, unpublished research, contracts.

Which tool, when

  • Amplify (vanderbilt.ai): university work, internal context. Reach for it first.
  • Copilot (copilot.microsoft.com, VU sign-in): work already inside Word, Outlook, Excel, Teams.
  • ChatGPT (chat.openai.com): public-level content, speed and breadth.
  • Claude / Perplexity / Gamma: long documents / cited research / slide decks. Green-level content only.

Trust, but verify

  • Fact, date, number, name, citation: check the source. Models invent realistic-looking ones.
  • Summary of your document: spot-check two claims against the original.
  • Any draft: you're the editor. Nothing ships unread.
  • Remember: confident phrasing is not evidence. It predicts words; it doesn't know facts.

Your starter prompt, fill in the brackets

You are [role with expertise and disposition]. Context: [who this is for and what they need]. My task: [one specific deliverable and its job]. Please write it as [format: shape, tone, length].

Keep the time it saves

Decide, out loud, where the saved hour goes: deeper work, breathing room, or learning time. Watch for workload creep, always-on drift, and invisible checking labor. If work feels heavier, say so; renegotiating scope is a professional move. Support: Vanderbilt Employee Assistance & Work-Life resources.
